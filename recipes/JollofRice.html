Jollof Rice

Jollof rice is a popular West African dish made with rice, tomatoes, onions, and a variety of spices. It is often served as a main course and can be accompanied by various proteins such as chicken, fish, or beef. Jollof rice is known for its vibrant red color and rich flavor, which comes from the combination of ingredients used in its preparation.

The recipe for jollof rice can vary depending on the region and personal preferences, but it typically includes ingredients such as long-grain rice, tomatoes, onions, bell peppers, garlic, ginger, and a blend of spices like thyme, bay leaves, and chili powder. Some variations may also include vegetables like carrots and peas.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups long-grain rice
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon grated ginger
  • 5 big (blended) tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 5-9 blendedchili pepper (adjust to taste)
  • Salt to taste
  • 4 cups chicken broth or water
  • Optional: diced bell peppers, carrots, peas

Steps

  1. Rinse the rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Set aside.
  2. In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat.
  3. Add the chopped onion and sauté until translucent.
  4. Add the minced garlic and grated ginger, and cook for another minute until fragrant.
  5. Stir in the blended tomatoes, blended chili peppers and tomato paste, and cook for about 10-15 minutes until the sauce thickens.
  6. Add the thyme, curry powder, bay leaves and salt. Stir to combine.
  7. Add the rinsed rice to the pot and stir to coat the rice with the tomato sauce.
  8. Pour in the chicken broth or water, and bring to a boil.
  9. Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and let it simmer for about 20-25 minutes, or until the rice is cooked and the liquid is absorbed.
  10. If using, add the diced bell peppers, carrots, and peas during the last 10 minutes of cooking.
  11. Remove the pot from heat and let it sit, covered, for an additional 5 minutes.
  12. Fluff the rice with a fork and serve with your choice of protein.
