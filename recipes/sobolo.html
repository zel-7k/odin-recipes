Sobolo

Sobolo, also known as Zobo, is a popular West African beverage made from the dried calyces of the hibiscus flower. It is known for its vibrant red color and refreshing taste. Sobolo is often enjoyed chilled and can be sweetened with sugar or honey to enhance its flavor. It is commonly served at social gatherings, parties, and special occasions in many West African countries.

The recipe for sobolo is quite simple and can be made with a few basic ingredients. The main ingredient is the dried hibiscus calyces, which are steeped in hot water to extract their flavor and color. Other ingredients may include ginger, cloves, cinnamon, and sweeteners like sugar or honey.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup dried hibiscus calyces
  • 4 cups water
  • 1-2 inches of fresh ginger, peeled and sliced
  • 2-3 cloves
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • Peels of a whole Pineapple
  • Sugar or honey to taste

Steps

  1. Rinse the dried hibiscus calyces under cold water to remove any impurities.
  2. In a large pot, bring the water to a boil.
  3. Add the rinsed hibiscus calyces, pineapple peels, sliced ginger, cloves, and cinnamon stick to the boiling water.
  4. Reduce the heat and let it simmer for about 15-20 minutes until the liquid turns a deep red color.
  5. Remove the pot from heat and let it cool for a few minutes.
  6. Strain the mixture through a fine mesh sieve or cheesecloth to remove the solids.
  7. Stir in sugar or honey to taste while the liquid is still warm, ensuring it dissolves completely.
  8. Allow the sobolo to cool to room temperature, then refrigerate until chilled.
  9. Serve the sobolo over ice.
